Our Ethos

Harmony between garden, landscape
and home

"A garden should feel inevitable — as though it could only ever have existed in precisely this place."

At Garden, Space & Soul, we believe that great garden design begins not just with the plants or the plans, but with deep listening. To the land, to the light, to the architecture, and to the people who will inhabit the space.


Every garden we create seeks holistic harmony — a seamless conversation between the structure of the design, the richness of the planting, the character of the surrounding landscape, and the spirit of the house it serves. Whether drawing from the formal traditions of English garden design or crafting something quietly contemporary, our work is always rooted in place.

Planting Philosophy

A rich, considered
plant palette

Planting is the living soul of a garden. We work with an exceptionally detailed knowledge of plant selection — choosing varieties not merely for their beauty, but for their suitability to the local soil, the microclimate, the light, and the character of the wider landscape.


From the deep horticultural traditions of the English country garden to contemporary naturalistic planting inspired by the new perennial movement, our palette spans structured formality and abundant informality — often within a single design.

Rosa 'Generous Gardener' Climbing rose — limestone soils
Verbascum olympicum Structural accent — dry chalk
Deschampsia cespitosa Naturalistic grass — damp clay
Alchemilla mollis Cottage edge — all soils
Betula utilis 'Jacquemontii' Specimen tree — winter structure
✦
Soil & Condition Led

Every plant choice begins with the soil. The region's varied geology — from Somerset's heavy clays to the free-draining limestone of the Cotswolds — shapes every scheme from the ground upward.

✦
Structured & Informal

We are equally at home designing precisely clipped topiary and formal knot gardens as we are composing the loose abundance of a traditional English border — and at our best, blending the two.

✦
Contemporary & Traditional

An intimate understanding of both the new perennial movement and the deep lineage of English horticulture allows us to design gardens that feel simultaneously timeless and entirely of the present.

How We Work

A design process built
on listening and craft

01
The First Conversation

Every project begins with a conversation — about your home, your aspirations, how you live outdoors, and what you feel is missing. No forms, no questionnaires. Just listening.

02
Site Visit & Analysis

Andrew visits the site to understand the soil, the light, the levels, the views, and the character of the place. This foundation shapes everything that follows.

03
Concept & Design

A design concept is developed — spatial, atmospheric, and specific to the place. Refined through conversation until it feels exactly right, then developed into full plans and planting schemes.

04
Delivery & Legacy

We guide the design through construction and planting, ensuring that the care taken at the drawing stage is honoured in the making of the garden itself.

About Andrew

A designer who
understands the land

Andrew Alden founded Garden, Space & Soul with a simple conviction: that the finest gardens emerge from a profound understanding of place. His work is shaped by a deep knowledge of English horticulture, a trained eye for spatial design, and a genuine love of the landscape of southern England.

Working across Somerset, Wiltshire, and the Cotswolds, Andrew brings an equally nuanced understanding of the formal traditions of country house garden design and the rich informality of the English cottage and kitchen garden — finding in both an opportunity for beauty, calm, and meaning.

His approach is personal and unhurried. Every client receives his singular, undivided attention. Every garden is designed from scratch, rooted entirely in the particular character of the site and the people who will inhabit it.
